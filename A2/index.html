Opioid Overdose Death in United State

America has high percentage of drug-related deaths in the world, according to a study released by National Safety Council accidental opioid overdoses death is more likely than a car accident for american, nowaday. The life expectancy rate is going down as the overdose death rate rises. This is a reminder that people should start paying attention to opioid crisis. Overdoses can be intentional or accidental. Overdoses of illegal drugs or even prescription drugs can also be fatal.

Overdose Death Rate Across United State in Year 2014

The choropleth map below demonstrates the number of death per 100,000 persons in each state in the USA.

In 2014, the states with the highest drug related overdose death were West Virginia with 34 death per 100,000 person, New Mexico with 26 death per 100,000 person, Kentucky and New Hampshine with 25 death per 100,000 person and Ohio with 24 death per 100,000 person.

U.S. Overdose Death by State (1999-2014)

Using the slider below to visualize how the number of death in each state changes over time.

Due to bigger population size, California, Texas, New York and Florida have higher number of overdose death cases. Apart from the big population size, we can see Ohio did not have significant population growth, but the number of overdose death case have a sharp increase in Ohio compare to other states in the U.S..

Opioid Overdose Deaths by Gender over Year

The overdose death rate is calculated by number of overdose death/population*100,000, the overdose death rate increases overtime, and Male tend to have higher rate than Female.

Research shows that different gender often use drugs differently and have different response to drugs, men have higher rates of dependence on illicit drugs which are more fatal and overwhelmed by substances, this also increases the death rate.
